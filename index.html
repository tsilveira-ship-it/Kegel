Technique

Identify & activate the PC muscle

1

Locate the muscle

Try stopping urination midstream. The muscle that contracts is the pubococcygeus. Only do this once for identification.

2

Isolate the contraction

Once identified, contract without engaging your abs, glutes or thighs. They must stay relaxed.

3

Breathe normally

Never hold your breath during the exercise. If you do, you are compensating with your abs.

4

Practice regularly

3 sessions per day, every day. The release is just as important as the contraction.

Tips

The golden rules

Consistency over intensity

Better 3 mini-sessions every day than one long session once a week.

Practice anywhere

At the office, on the bus, watching TV... No one can tell you're training.

Empty your bladder first

Always practice with an empty bladder to avoid discomfort and infection risk.

Patience & progression

First results between 4 and 6 weeks. Clear improvements at 3 months.

Don't hold your breath

Breathe normally during contraction. Apnea engages the abs.

Don't clench your glutes

If your buttocks tighten, you're not isolating the right muscle.

Don't overdo it

Overtraining fatigues the muscle and causes the opposite effect.

Don't practice while urinating

Stopping the flow should only be a test. Doing it regularly causes problems.
